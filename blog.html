About Us





Story

We treasure coffee. Coffee is a huge part of our lives and the majority of our family and friends are in a job that involves coffee. The amazing thing is my family is involved in every part of the coffee making process. However, in their process of making coffee my family is not alone.They are surrounded by people who also share the same interests as they and are passionate in what they are doing.

My name is Sisay, and I am part of the Guga family. My family lives in the Sidama part of Ethiopia, and we grow coffee. The whole process started with my grandfather Guga who had a small coffee farm in Aleta Chuko County. My grandfather would take care of his farm daily and make sure the coffee beans grew properly. After the coffee beans ripened, he then would sell the coffee to the local residents

My father Gunsamo grew up admiring all the things that his father was doing with the coffee business, and he also wished to continue the process and make it more effective and smoother than it was before. In the town of Aleta Chuko there was a coffee farmers union which was hiring and luckily my dad had the opportunity to get his first job there. While working there, he had a direct relationship with the coffee farmers, and he saw how the coffee beans were washed and processed at the washing station. After going through this process and learning how everything worked, my dad decided to also open his own coffee bean washing station.

One day a problem occurred to me as I was at the washing station. I realized that my family was unable to sell the coffee internationally because there wasn’t a direct export process from the washing station. There needed to be something done so we could expand our business and not just restrict it to one country. After a few months I had to come to America to also continue the coffee process and set up a direct trading system. I worked at a coffee shop as a barista, and there I was able to learn and see how Americans enjoy coffee. Also, I went to City College of San Francisco and majored in International Business.

We the Guga family have been in every aspect of the coffee business: we have been farmers, we have been owners of coffee bean washing stations, and we have seen how consumers of coffee from every walks of life enjoy it, We have been through all these processes and we do these things in the hopes that every person that drinks our coffee has a good experience and that the coffee is of the highest quality. My dad is able to export coffee directly from the washing station. I have started to import our family’s coffee into the United States. We truly love what we do, and we want that to show in our coffee.
